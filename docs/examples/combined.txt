Unit 5 - Travel English

Vocabulary

abroad - fora do país, no exterior
departure - partida, saída
arrival - chegada
customs - alfândega
boarding pass - cartão de embarque
layover - escala, parada técnica entre voos
carry-on - bagagem de mão
check-in - processo de registro no aeroporto ou hotel
jet lag - cansaço causado pela diferença de fuso horário
round trip - ida e volta

Reading: Life Abroad

Moving to another country is one of the most challenging experiences a person can have. At first, everything feels exciting - new food, new people, new language. But after a few weeks, many people experience what is called "culture shock." They miss their family, their routine, and even simple things like familiar food or their favorite TV shows.

The key to adapting is staying open-minded. Try to learn at least basic phrases in the local language. People appreciate the effort, even when you make mistakes. Join local groups or activities to meet people outside of work or school.

Most people who live abroad say it changed them in ways they never expected. They became more independent, more tolerant, and better at solving problems on their own.


Exercise 1 - Complete the sentences (with answer key)

1. I always take a ______ bag on short trips so I don't have to wait at baggage claim.
Answer: carry-on

2. Her flight had a three-hour ______ in Miami before continuing to São Paulo.
Answer: layover

3. After a 14-hour flight, he was exhausted from ______.
Answer: jet lag

4. You need to show your ______ at the gate before boarding.
Answer: boarding pass

5. She lived ______ for two years before moving back to Brazil.
Answer: abroad
